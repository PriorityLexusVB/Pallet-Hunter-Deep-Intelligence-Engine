Buyer Checklist (printable)

Local deals (before you pay)
  • Power-on test (no grinding / smoke smell)
  • Confirm what’s included (battery/charger/case/blades)
  • Check cracks, missing screws, bent parts
  • Battery: no swelling, no corrosion
  • Serial/model label present
  • Quick comp check (eBay “sold”)
Pallets / auctions (before you bid)
  • Manifest clarity (models + counts)
  • Assume some dead units — price that in
  • Test everything within 48 hours
  • Bundle/parts exit plan for Tier C
Golden rule If you can’t test it and the seller won’t allow it, your price must drop into “parts money” — or you walk.

Seller Checklist (how to sell fast)

Photos (8–12)
  • Full tool, both sides
  • Model label/serial
  • Battery + charger included
  • Close-ups of wear areas
  • Show damage (honest)
Listing formula Title: BRAND + VOLT + MODEL + WHAT’S INCLUDED
Example: “Milwaukee M18 Fuel Impact + 2 Batteries + Charger”
Pricing: use eBay “sold comps”, then price slightly under local comps for speed.
Velocity rule Inventory sitting is profit leaking. List fast.

1) Category Overview — Power Tools

What this category is Corded + cordless drills, impacts, saws, grinders, sanders, nailers — plus batteries/chargers/cases.
Why it’s profitable Constant demand + durability + brand ecosystems. Used tools still provide value.
How common in liquidation Extremely common via returns/overstock/shelf pulls from home improvement & big-box channels.
Who sells undervalued Retailers clearing space, government surplus, contractors/estates moving fast, shop closures.

2) Item Tiers

Tier A — high value / fast flips Resale: ~$300–$1,000+. Examples: Milwaukee M18 Fuel kits, DeWalt XR kits, Makita brushless kits, Hilti/Festool, table/miter saws.
Tier B — steady movers Resale: ~$50–$300. Examples: individual drills/impacts, circular saws, grinders, sanders, corded tools.
Tier C — low value / bundles Resale: ~$5–$50 each (best bundled). Examples: accessories, partial kits, old/unknown brands, parts units.

3) ROI Map

Tier A
  • Buy: $100–$300 effective/unit
  • Sell: $300–$1,000+
  • ROI: ~50–150%
  • Flip: 7–21 days
  • Failure: 5–15%
  • Repairs: minimal (battery/charger)
Tier B
  • Buy: $20–$100
  • Sell: $50–$300
  • ROI: ~30–80%
  • Flip: 10–30 days
  • Failure: 10–30%
  • Repairs: occasional (cord/switch/brushes)
Tier C
  • Buy: $5–$30
  • Sell: $10–$150 (bundles)
  • ROI: ~20–50% (riskier)
  • Flip: 14–60+ days
  • Failure: up to 50%
  • Strategy: bundle/parts/wholesale exit

4) Local Demand — Virginia Beach

Demand drivers Military relocation, contractors, DIY homeowners, auto/mechanics, students (bundle buyers).
Seasonality Peak: March–May. Slow: Dec–Feb. Best velocity: Fri–Sun pickups.
Best platforms Facebook Marketplace + OfferUp (speed). eBay for high value / national buyers.

5) Best Acquisition Sources (ranked)

  1. B-Stock — consistent pallets/returns; undervalued due to bulk clearing.
  2. DirectLiquidation — retailer returns/overstock; priced to move.
  3. Liquidation.com — mixed pallets; requires strict manifest discipline.
  4. GovDeals — surplus tools sold in lots; value often missed.
  5. Facebook Marketplace — distress sellers + contractor cleanouts.
  6. Craigslist — garage/estate lots from older demographic sellers.
  7. OfferUp — fast “today” deals; good for bundles.
  8. AuctionZip / estate auctions — workshop liquidations; preview matters.

6) Triggers / Signals

High-value phrases
moving — must sell today closing business contractor retiring garage cleanout estate sale — tools remodel / upgrading everything must go bundle price

7) Negotiation Guide

Best opening line “I can pick up today and pay cash — is it available?”
Anchor Missing parts / untested status justify a lower offer.
When to go in person Any time value is $100+.
Inspect Power test, battery safety, cracks, accessories, serial label.

8) Red Flags (Do Not Buy)

  • Does not power on (unless parts-priced)
  • Swollen/leaking battery (safety)
  • Missing charger/battery on cordless systems (value collapse)
  • Cracked housing / structural damage
  • Seller refuses testing
  • Inconsistent or changing details

9) Repair / Refurb (only what’s worth it)

Common issues Dead batteries, missing chargers, cords, switches, worn brushes (older).
Rules Repairs ≤ 20% of expected resale. If fix takes > 60 minutes, sell as-is or part out.

Business Plan — Executive Summary

Pallet Hunter is an asset-backed resale engine focused on power tools. We acquire undervalued inventory from liquidation channels and motivated local sellers, verify function, do light refurbishment, and resell through optimized channels. The business is driven by repeatable cycles (7–30 days) with strict risk controls.

Business Plan — Business Model

  • Acquisition: local deals first; pallets later. Target buy: 30–50% of equivalent retail value.
  • Processing: 24–48h intake SLA; test checklist; clean + photograph.
  • Sales routing: local speed (FB/OfferUp) + online margin (eBay) + bundles for Tier C.
  • Capital loop: recycle capital quickly; prioritize cycle time over “perfect” margin.

Business Plan — Unit Economics

Conservative example cycle Buy $2,000 → Sell $5,200 → Repairs $300 → Fees/Ops $400 → Net ~$2,500 (ROI ~125%)

Business Plan — Risk Control

  • Only buy what can be tested (or tested within 48h of intake for pallets).
  • No battery safety risks. No structural damage.
  • Parts/bundle exit plan for imperfect inventory.
  • Diversify channels (not platform-dependent).

Business Plan — Scaling

Phase 1 — Solo Local deals • $500–$2,000 capital • learn SOP
Phase 2 — Volume Add helper(s) • 2–4 pallets/month • faster intake/listing
Phase 3 — Multi-City Replicate SOP • optional app for scoring + routing + alerts
