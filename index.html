1) Category Overview — Power Tools

        
    
          
  • What it is: Corded + cordless power tools (drills, impacts, saws, grinders, sanders) and supporting ecosystem (batteries, chargers, cases, accessories).
    • 
          
  • Why profitable: Year-round demand (DIY + contractors + auto). Durable goods hold resale value; premium brands are highly liquid.
    • 
          
  • Common in liquidation: Retail returns, shelf pulls, and overstock are frequent in home improvement liquidation channels.
    • 
          
  • Typical sellers: Retailers (returns), government surplus, contractors retiring, estates/garage cleanouts, small shop closures.
    • 
          
  • Why undervalued: Sellers optimize for speed/space recovery, not market pricing; many items are lightly used or cosmetic-only.
    • 
        

      


      

        
2) Item Tiers


        

          
            
Tier A — High value / low volume / fast flips

            
Expand/Collapse

          
          

            
Examples: Milwaukee M18 Fuel kits, DeWalt 20V combo kits, Makita brushless kits, Hilti/Festool, jobsite table saws, miter saws.

            
Resale ranges: ~$300–$1,000+ depending on kit completeness and condition.

            
Why it moves: Contractors pay for known systems and want immediate availability.

          

        


        

          
            
Tier B — Medium value / steady movers

            
Expand/Collapse

          
          

            
Examples: individual drills/impacts, circular saws, grinders, sanders, nailers, mid-tier tool-only listings.

            
Resale ranges: ~$50–$300.

            
Why it moves: DIY buyers + trades needing a backup tool or quick replacement.

          

        


        

          
            
Tier C — Lower value / bundle items

            
Expand/Collapse

          
          

            
Examples: accessories, mixed lots, older/unknown brands, partial kits, “for parts” units.

            
Resale ranges: ~$5–$50 each, best as bundles ($25–$150 lots).

            
Why it moves: Budget buyers + parts buyers + “starter kit” bundles.

7) Negotiation Guide

        

          
    
            
  • Opening: “I can pick up today and pay cash — is it available?”
    • 
            
  • Anchor: Start at ~30–40% under fair used price (leave room to move).
    • 
            
  • Frame: “I’m taking the risk on testing/repairs — can you do $X if I grab it now?”
    • 
            
  • Go in person: anytime value is $100+ or seller claims “works great.”
    • 
            
  • Bring cash: always for local deals; it closes faster.
    • 
            
  • Avoid flakes: confirm time/address; “I’m leaving now” cadence.
    • 
            
  • Close quickly: bundle offers; solve their problem (space/time).
    • 
            
  • Inspect before buying: power-on test, battery health, charger included, cracks, unusual smells, serial label present.
    • 
          

        

      


      

        
8) Red Flags (Do Not Buy)

        
    
          
  • Does not power on (unless priced as parts)
    • 
          
  • Swollen/leaking battery (safety risk)
    • 
          
  • Missing charger/battery on cordless systems (value collapse)
    • 
          
  • Cracked housing / structural damage
    • 
          
  • Seller won’t allow testing
    • 
          
  • Manifest/listing keeps changing; inconsistent details
    • 
          
  • “Too good to be true” pricing without verification
    • 
        

      


      

        
9) Repair / Refurb Guide (Only What’s Worth It)

        

          
    
            
  • Common issues: dead batteries, missing chargers, worn brushes (older), bad switches, damaged cords.
    • 
            
  • Cost rule: repairs ≤ 20% of expected resale.
    • 
            
  • Time rule: if fix takes >45–60 min, usually sell as-is or part out (unless Tier A).
    • 
            
  • Tools needed: basic drivers, multimeter, compressed air, cleaning supplies, test battery/charger, extension cord.
    • 
            
  • Worth it: Tier A + strong brands. Tier C repairs usually lose money.
    • 
          

        

      


      
      

        
Business Plan — Executive Summary

        

          Pallet Hunter is an asset-backed resale engine focused on power tools. We buy undervalued inventory from liquidation
          channels + motivated local sellers, test/clean/light refurb, and sell through the fastest channel for each tier.
          Core advantage: disciplined sourcing + ROI gates + fast inventory turns (7–30 days typical).
        

      


      

        
Business Plan — Model

        
    
          
  • Acquisition: pallets/returns + local deals. Target buy: 30–50% of retail equivalent.
    • 
          
  • Processing: 24–48h intake SLA; test checklist; clean + photograph.
    • 
          
  • Sales routing: local speed (FB/OfferUp) + online margin (eBay) + bundles for low tier.
    • 
          
  • Capital loop: reinvest weekly; prioritize cycle time.
    • 
        

      


      

        
Business Plan — Unit Economics

        

          
Conservative example: Buy $2,000 → Sell $5,200 → Repairs $300 → Fees/ops $400 → Net ~$2,500 (ROI ~125%).

          
The “money” is not a single jackpot — it’s repeatable cycles + strict no-buy rules.

        

      


      

        
Business Plan — Risk Control

        
    
          
  • Only buy what can be tested (or tested within 48h of pallet intake).
    • 
          
  • No battery safety risks. No structural damage.
    • 
          
  • Parts/bundle exit plan for imperfect inventory.
    • 
          
  • Diversified channels (not platform-dependent).
    • 
        

      


      

        
Business Plan — Scaling

        
    
          
  • Phase 1: Solo operator, $5k–$15k capital, weekly cycles.
    • 
          
  • Phase 2: Add helper(s), 2–4 pallets/month, delegated testing/listing.
    • 
          
  • Phase 3: Multi-city SOP replication; optional app for deal scoring + ops routing.
    •

Capital Ask Options (Copy/Paste)

        

          

            
Option A — Revenue Share Until Return

            
Investor funds inventory. Net profits split until principal + agreed return is repaid, then renegotiate or exit.

          

          

            
Option B — Fixed Return Per Cycle (Asset-backed)

            
Investor receives fixed % return per 30 days, supported by inventory and sales records.

          

          

            
Option C — Equity + Cashflow Hybrid

            
Smaller equity stake with ongoing cashflow participation from operations.

          

          
Note: final terms should be reviewed with counsel for compliance in your state.
